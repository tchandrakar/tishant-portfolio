Design Delivery Dispatch & Routing (batching, courier assignment, ETA)

Very Hard food-delivery ride-sharing maps-location

1. Problem statement

Design the dispatch engine: given a stream of new orders and a fleet of couriers continuously reporting position, assign orders to couriers (possibly in batches of 2–3) to minimise customer ETA + courier idle time + total drive distance, subject to constraints (food temperature, alcohol licensing, vehicle capacity, courier preferences). This is the brain of any food-delivery platform and is the most algorithmically dense component.

2. Functional requirements

  • Ingest order events (new, ready, cancelled) from Order Service.
  • Ingest courier pings (location, status, battery) at 1 Hz.
  • Decide assignment: single or batched, including pickup/drop sequence.
  • Push offer to courier; handle accept/reject within SLA.
  • Continuously re-estimate ETA and re-assign if a better plan exists pre-pickup.
  • Emit assignment events for tracking, payouts, analytics.

3. Non-functional requirements

  • Latency: assignment decision < 2 s p99 from "food ready" event.
  • Throughput: 10 K assignments/min peak; 1 M open orders steady-state globally.
  • Quality: < 5% reject rate; < 4 min ETA error p50.
  • Availability: 99.95% — falling back to greedy if the optimiser is down.

4. Capacity estimation

  • Couriers: 600 K active × 1 Hz pings = 600 K msg/s, ~120 MB/s into Kafka.
  • Orders: peak 1 K orders/s; each triggers ~1 KB assignment record.
  • Index size: live courier index ~500 MB in Redis Geo (sharded by region).
  • Compute: VRP solver budget ~200 ms × 100 regions × 12 ticks/min = 240 CPU-min/min ≈ 240 cores.

5. API design

POST /v1/dispatch/order      {orderId, restLat, restLng, dropLat, dropLng, prepEta, constraints}
POST /v1/dispatch/courier    {courierId, lat, lng, status, battery}   (internal stream)
GET  /v1/dispatch/order/{id}/assignment                              → {courierId, etaSec}
POST /v1/dispatch/offer/{id}/accept|reject  {courierId}              → ack

6. High-level architecture

[Order Svc]──┐ ↓ Kafka(order.events) [Courier app]→[Geo Ingest]→Kafka(courier.pings)→[Redis GEO + Flink state] ↓ ↑ [Region Sharder]──────┐ │ ↓ │ [Dispatch Worker (per region)] ↓ ┌──── [VRP Solver / OR-Tools] ────┐ │ │ [ETA Svc] ← [Routing: OSRM] ← [Live traffic] ↓ [Assignment Store (Cassandra)] → Kafka(assign.events) ↓ [Tracking, Payouts, Analytics]

Each region (H3 res-6 cell or city) is owned by exactly one dispatch worker leader (via etcd/ZooKeeper). State is rebuildable from Kafka + Redis on failover.

7. Data model & storage

7.1 Live courier index (Redis)

GEOADD region:bom:active <lng> <lat> courier:42
HSET courier:42 status free orders 0 battery 81 vehicle bike

7.2 Open orders (in-memory per worker, snapshot to Redis)

{ orderId, restLoc, dropLoc, prepReadyAt, deadline, batchKey }

7.3 Assignments (Cassandra)

Partition region+date, clustering created_at; trail used for ML training and dispute resolution.

8. Detailed component design

8.1 Region sharding

Each H3 res-6 hex (~36 km²) is a region; consistent-hashed to dispatch workers; one leader per region with a hot standby. Boundaries are widened to overlap so couriers near edges are visible to both regions.

8.2 Solver loop

Every 2 s tick: snapshot open orders + couriers within region. Build candidate assignments — singletons + batched pairs whose pickup-delta < 5 min and drops are along-route (cosine of bearings > 0.7). Cost = α·customerETA + β·courierIdleness + γ·extraDistance − δ·courierPay. Solve as min-cost flow (OR-Tools); cap at 500 nodes per tick.

8.3 Offer protocol

Push to courier app via persistent connection; 30 s to accept; on reject, mark cool-down 60 s and re-solve. Track historical accept-rate as a feature.

8.4 ETA service

OSRM gives free-flow time on the road graph; ML residual model adds traffic + weather + restaurant prep variance. Updates every 10 s while in-flight.

8.5 Re-assignment

Pre-pickup, if a better plan saves > 90 s and courier hasn't accepted, swap. Post-pickup is locked except for catastrophic failures.

9. Scaling & bottlenecks

  • Hot region (downtown Friday 8 PM): sub-shard H3 to res-7; spin up extra solver pods.
  • Solver SLA: hard timeout 200 ms — fall back to greedy nearest-neighbor with batching heuristic.
  • Ping flood: drop to 0.2 Hz when courier is idle > 5 min; full rate when assigned.
  • Leader failover: Kafka offset-based recovery; warm Redis snapshot every 30 s.
  • Cross-region orders (suburb → downtown): handled by larger "metro" region overlaying the hex grid.

12. Talking points for the interviewer

  • "I'd treat dispatch as a stream-of-decisions problem on top of Kafka — every assignment is reproducible from inputs."
  • "The most underrated lever is the delay before assignment: waiting 30 s to batch can outperform instant dispatch on unit economics."
  • "For correctness, region leadership via consensus is non-negotiable; I never want two workers double-assigning a courier."
  • "Greedy fallback is a feature, not a hack — the system must keep moving when the optimiser is unhealthy."

Every technology choice in this design exists for a concrete reason.
