Design Cold-start Recommender (new users / new items)

Hard recommendation

1. Problem statement

Design the cold-start path for a recommender that serves both new users (no history) and new items (no engagement signal) without degrading the For You experience. Targets: 1B users with ~5M new signups/day, 500M items with ~5M new uploads/day, sub-100 ms recommendation latency at 580K QPS, while ramping new items toward steady-state distribution within hours, not days.

2. Functional requirements

  • For new user: produce a personalized first-session feed using onboarding signals (locale, language, interests, device).
  • For new item: ensure exposure to a diverse exploration audience to gather initial labels.
  • Smooth handoff from cold-start path to steady-state two-tower stack as signal accrues.
  • Honor safety filters (age, region, integrity) on cold items.
  • Allow per-segment policies (e.g. teens get different exploration mix).

3. Non-functional requirements

  • Latency: p99 under 100 ms even on the cold path.
  • Time-to-warm: a new user has full personalization within 10 sessions; a new item has reliable CTR estimate within 1000 impressions.
  • Coverage: at least 80% of new items receive 100 impressions in the first 24 hours.
  • Availability: 99.95%; cold-start fallback always available.

4. Capacity estimation

  • New users: 5M/day -> 60/sec; first-session feed served from a precomputed catalog of trending-by-locale (1000 items per locale * 200 locales = 200K total).
  • New items/day: 5M; need average 100 free impressions each = 500M exploration impressions/day = 5800/sec, ~3% of total feed traffic.
  • Content embeddings on upload: 5M items * 200ms GPU = 1000 GPU-seconds, ~12 GPU nodes peak.
  • Cold-item index: 5M items * 2 days kept hot = 10M-vector "fresh" index; HNSW 5 GB.

5. API design

POST /v1/recs/feed   {userId, ctx}     -> [items]
   (router decides cold vs warm path)
POST /v1/onboarding/interests  {userId, topicIds[]}
GET  /v1/explore/budget/{itemId}       -> {remaining_impressions}
POST /v1/coldstart/score   {userId, itemId} -> score    (internal)

6. High-level architecture

[Rec Orchestrator] | cold? -----+----- warm? | | v v [Cold-start path] [Two-tower + ranker] | +-- [Onboarding feature builder] +-- [Content-based ANN (item-item)] +-- [Trending-by-locale cache] +-- [Exploration bandit (Thompson)] | v [Safety filter] -> top-N -> client Item ingest: [Upload] -> [Content emb svc (GPU)] -> [Fresh ANN index] | +-> [Explore quota mgr] (impressions budget per item)

Walk-through: orchestrator inspects user signal (impressions in last 7d). If below threshold, takes cold path: builds embedding from declared interests + content-based item ANN + trending-by-locale, plus exploration injection. Otherwise warm path. Both paths share safety filter.

7. Data model & storage

7.1 Onboarding profile (Redis)

KEY  onboard:user:{userId}   VAL {locale, langs[], topicIds[], device, signupTs}
                              TTL 30 days (or until promoted to full profile)

7.2 Trending-by-locale (precomputed)

KEY  trending:{locale}:{topic}  VAL [itemId,score][] top-1000  TTL 15 min
Job: streaming top-K maintained in Flink with sliding 1-hour window.

7.3 Exploration budget (Cassandra)

CREATE TABLE explore_budget (
  item_id uuid PRIMARY KEY,
  budget_remaining counter,         -- decrement on impression
  cold_until timestamp);
TTL 48h after item upload.

8. Detailed component design

8.1 New-user path

User embedding bootstrapped as average of clicked-interest topic embeddings. Retrieval: content-based ANN (item content tower trained from hashtags/audio/visual, no engagement signal needed). Re-ranked by a meta-learning model (MAML-flavored) trained to fit thin-history users in a few gradient steps. Switches to standard two-tower after 50 engagement events.

8.2 New-item path

Two-stage exploration. Stage 1 (first 100 impressions): random injection into ~5% of feed slots for matched-locale users. Stage 2 (next 900 impressions): Thompson-sampling bandit on the cold item with prior from content-based predicted CTR. After 1000 impressions, item enters main two-tower index.

8.3 Handoff logic

Per-user cold flag falls when (engagements >= 50) AND (sessions >= 5). Per-item cold flag falls when (impressions >= 1000) OR (age > 48h). A shadow log compares cold-path predictions to warm-path on borderline users to detect handoff regressions.

9. Scaling & bottlenecks

  • Bot abuse of exploration budget: rate-limit, weight by user trust score; otherwise spammers steal cold-start surface.
  • Locale skew: long-tail locales have thin trending caches; fall back to language-level.
  • Onboarding bias: declared interests rarely predict real engagement; weight engagement signals stronger after first session.
  • Fairness: exploration budget must not over-favor large creators; per-creator cap of 1000 free impressions/day.
  • Latency on cold path: content-based ANN over full corpus is expensive; restrict to fresh + trending shards.

12. Talking points for the interviewer

  • "Cold-start is two problems with one name. New users need warm content; new items need warm audiences. The pipelines are different."
  • "Free exploration is a tax the warm system pays so the catalog stays alive. I'd budget it explicitly, around 3-5% of feed slots."
  • "The biggest mistake is conflating onboarding-stated interests with engagement signal. They diverge fast."

13. Tech stack & rationale

Every technology choice in this design exists for a concrete reason.

TechnologyWhy we chose it (for THIS problem)Alternatives considered
HNSW (fresh ANN index) Cold-item index is small (~10M vectors over 2 days kept hot, 5 GB) but needs >0.95 recall and zero training step so newly-uploaded items are searchable in minutes; HNSW's incremental insert is ideal vs IVF-PQ which requires periodic retrain on the centroids. FAISS IVF-PQ (centroid retrain hurts freshness); brute force (5 GB scan too slow at 580K QPS).
Content tower (no engagement signal) 5M new items/day have zero engagement labels on day 0; a tower trained on hashtags/audio/visual produces a usable embedding from upload metadata alone, the only viable retrieval source for items in their first 100 impressions. Collaborative filtering (no signal until labels arrive); pure popularity (kills personalization).
Thompson Sampling bandit Stage-2 exploration (impressions 100-1000) needs to converge a CTR estimate fast on each cold item; Thompson is regret-optimal with content-derived prior (Beta(alpha,beta) seeded from predicted CTR), beating epsilon-greedy on time-to-warm. Epsilon-greedy (wastes pulls); UCB (similar; TS picked for natural Bayesian prior integration).
MAML-style meta-learner New users with <50 events have too thin a history for the 200-feature DLRM ranker to fit; meta-learning trained to adapt in a few gradient steps gives reasonable scores on 5-event users where the warm model would overfit or default. Warm model with default features (severe quality drop); pure popularity (ignores onboarding signal).
Apache Flink (trending top-K) Trending-by-locale with sliding 1-hour window across 200 locales must refresh every 15 minutes; Flink's keyed sliding-window aggregation per (locale, topic) maintains top-1000 lists in stream with bounded state, cited per DDIA Ch.11. Spark Streaming (micro-batch coarser than 1-min refresh); Kafka Streams (sliding-window state larger).
Redis (onboarding profile + trending cache) 200 locales * 1000 trending items + 5M new users/day onboarding state: small footprint, sub-1ms read needed inside the 100ms cold-path budget. TTL 30 days on onboarding lets state auto-expire when promoted to full profile. Memcached (no native cluster mode); DynamoDB (10ms tail violates budget).
Cassandra (exploration budget) 5M new items/day each tracking impression budget with per-impression decrement at peak rates needs a counter primitive that tolerates partition tolerance over strict consistency; Cassandra counters with TTL 48h are a textbook fit per DDIA Ch.7. Redis INCR (no auto-TTL on counters cleanly); DynamoDB atomic counters (cost at this write rate).
Apache Kafka 5M new uploads/day stream into the embedding service; partitioned by item_id so the embedding GPU pool consumes evenly across 12 nodes during peak. Decision/reward events for the bandit also flow through Kafka for replay. Kinesis (per-shard cap); SQS (no replay, no partition order).
NVIDIA T4 GPUs (embedding service) 5M items/day * 200ms inference = 1000 GPU-seconds, 12 T4 nodes peak; T4's INT8 path doubles throughput vs FP16 for content-tower inference and is cost-optimal at this rate vs A100. A100 (overprovisioned for 60 items/sec); CPU (would need 300+ nodes).
Apache Spark (nightly retraining) Content tower and meta-learner retrain over the Iceberg event log nightly; Spark's wide shuffles handle the 30-day join between new-item content features and accrued engagement labels, per DDIA Ch.10. Beam (vendor lock); ad-hoc Python (cannot scale to PB-level joins).
Apache Iceberg on S3 Long-term event log for nightly content-tower training plus shadow-log comparisons at handoff boundaries; Iceberg's schema evolution lets us add new content features (audio fingerprints, etc.) without rewriting history. Raw Parquet (no atomic commits); Hudi (similar; Iceberg picked for engine neutrality).
Bloom filter (already-seen on cold path) Even cold-path users must not see duplicates; the per-user 12 KB bloom from the warm pipeline is reused so handoff is seamless and the cold path doesn't ship a parallel dedupe structure. Exact set (10x memory); no dedupe (hurts session metrics on returning cold users).
Per-creator rate limiter Exploration budget at 500M free impressions/day must not over-favor large creators; a token-bucket rate limiter at 1000 free impressions/day per creator enforces fairness without rejecting all of a hot creator's uploads. No cap (large-creator monopoly); strict equal split (under-utilizes budget on quality creators).
