Design YouTube (UGC video platform)

Hard video-streaming cdn recommendation search

1. Problem statement

Design a user-generated video platform where anyone can upload a clip and anyone can watch it. YouTube has ~2.7 B logged-in MAU, ~500 hours of video uploaded every minute, more than 1 B watch hours per day, and a search/discovery surface that drives ~70% of the watch time via recommendations. Unlike Netflix, the catalog is unbounded, the long-tail is enormous (~50% of views come from videos with < 100k lifetime views), and copyright/abuse moderation is a first-class concern.

2. Functional requirements

  • Upload a video (chunked, resumable), get a watch URL.
  • Stream playback (ABR) on web, mobile, TV; live streams & shorts as variants.
  • Search by query, channel, hashtag.
  • Personalized home feed and "Up Next" recommendations.
  • Likes, comments, subscriptions, watch later.
  • Content ID matching for copyright.

3. Non-functional requirements

  • Latency: search < 200 ms p95; playback start < 1.5 s p95.
  • Scale: 2.7 B MAU, peak ~50 M concurrent watchers, 500 hr/min ingest.
  • Availability: 99.95% playback.
  • Durability: 11 nines on uploaded originals.
  • Long tail friendly: the 99th percentile cold video must still play in under 3 s.

4. Capacity estimation

  • Ingest: 500 hr/min × 60 min = 30,000 hr/hr → ~720k h/day. At avg 1 GB/hr mezzanine, that's ~720 TB/day raw, ×10 for transcodes ≈ 7 PB/day.
  • QPS: 50 M concurrent / 5 s manifest refresh = 10 M manifest RPS; search ~1 M RPS at peak.
  • Storage: cumulative catalog > 5 EB across mezzanines + encodes.
  • Bandwidth: avg 3 Mbps × 50 M concurrent = 150 Tbps egress.
  • Telemetry: 50 M × 0.5 events/s = 25 M events/s into Pub/Sub.

6. High-level architecture

[Uploader] → [Resumable Upload Svc] → [GCS raw bucket] │ ▼ [Transcode workers (k8s)] │ [GCS encoded variants] │ ▼ [Viewer] → [Edge CDN (Google Global Cache)] → [Origin shield] → GCS Control plane: [Viewer] → [Frontend] → [Watch Svc] ─► [Video Meta (Spanner)] ├────► [Search (custom inverted index)] └────► [Reco Svc] ◄── [Feature store] ▲ [Pub/Sub: events] ─┘ → [Dataflow] → [BigQuery + Bigtable]

Hot videos live close to viewers in Google Global Cache (GGC) nodes inside ISPs. Cold long-tail videos fetch from a regional origin shield that absorbs misses before they hit GCS, preventing one viral comeback from causing a storage thundering herd.

7. Data model & storage

7.1 Video metadata (Spanner)

CREATE TABLE videos (
  video_id    STRING(36) PRIMARY KEY,
  channel_id  STRING(36),
  title       STRING(MAX),
  duration_s  INT64,
  status      STRING(16),   -- processing|ready|blocked
  visibility  STRING(16),
  created_at  TIMESTAMP
);
CREATE TABLE video_stats (
  video_id STRING(36),
  bucket_hour TIMESTAMP,
  views INT64, likes INT64, watch_ms INT64,
) PRIMARY KEY (video_id, bucket_hour);

7.2 Why Spanner?

Strong global consistency for monetization-critical state (privacy flags, monetization status, takedowns) with horizontal scale. View counts are kept in Bigtable + a stream aggregator because they're append-heavy and tolerate eventual consistency.

8. Detailed component design

8.1 Upload pipeline

Resumable HTTP uploads (RFC-style, chunked, with byte-range resume). Once finalized, a Pub/Sub message triggers transcoding into ~12 variants (codec ∈ {H.264, VP9, AV1} × bitrate × resolution). Thumbnails are extracted at scene-change points.

8.2 Search

Inverted index sharded by term-hash; within a shard, postings are stored in time-decay buckets so newer videos rank without rewriting the whole index. Query fan-out + scatter-gather aggregator merges shard results.

8.3 Recommendations

Two towers: candidate generator (deep retrieval over a billion items using ANN on learned embeddings) → ranker (multi-task DNN scoring CTR, watch-time, satisfaction). Trained daily on Dataflow over a year of engagement events. The ranker re-scores online with fresh features (last-N watched, time-of-day).

8.4 Content ID

Every uploaded video is fingerprinted (audio + perceptual video hashes) and matched against a reference DB; matches trigger monetization-share or takedown rules before the video goes public.

9. Scaling & bottlenecks

  • Viral spike: tier-1 GGC absorbs 90%+; tier-2 regional shield protects origin.
  • Long tail: origin must serve the 0.001%-popularity videos; we keep them on object storage + small SSD warm cache, accept 2.5 s startup.
  • Comments hot partitions: shard by (video_id, bucket) where bucket = floor(comment_id / 10k); aggregator merges across buckets at read time.
  • Search index churn: incremental segment merge during off-peak; LSM-style.

12. Talking points for the interviewer

  • "I'd treat the event log (uploads, plays, likes) as the source of truth and derive search, recommendations, and analytics from it — that's the only way the read paths stay decoupled."
  • "The hardest part is the long tail — popularity follows a Zipf curve so the top 1% of videos drive 80% of bytes; my system has to be cheap enough to also serve the bottom 99%."
  • "For Content ID I'd lean on perceptual hashes pre-publish, then a slower forensic match async; otherwise upload latency tanks."
