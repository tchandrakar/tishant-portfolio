Design Hotstar / JioCinema (live + VOD with 25M+ concurrent)

Very Hard video-streaming cdn

1. Problem statement

Design a streaming platform that handles both VOD and live events, where the live events are cricket matches (IPL final). Hotstar reached 25.3 M concurrent viewers in 2019 and JioCinema crossed 32 M concurrent in the 2023 IPL final — one of the largest single-event audiences in internet history. The system must scale from a normal day's ~5 M concurrent VOD viewers to 30 M+ concurrent live viewers within a 2-hour window, with sub-30-second glass-to-glass latency to keep live commentary in sync with social chatter.

2. Functional requirements

  • Live linear streaming of an event with adaptive bitrate.
  • VOD catalog (movies, shows) browse + playback.
  • Multi-language audio + multi-camera feeds.
  • DVR rewind during live (last 2 hours).
  • Real-time chat / reactions during the live event.
  • Geo-restrictions per content rights.

3. Non-functional requirements

  • Scale: 30 M peak concurrent on a single live event; ramp from 1 M → 30 M within 30 minutes after toss.
  • Latency: < 30 s glass-to-glass for live; VOD start < 2 s.
  • Availability: 99.99% during the event window — failure is news.
  • Bandwidth: tens of Tbps in-country (India).
  • Cost: ARPU is low (free or ad-supported), so per-viewer cost must be aggressively optimized.

4. Capacity estimation

  • Bandwidth: 30 M × avg 2.5 Mbps (mobile-dominant) = 75 Tbps egress.
  • Manifest fetches: 30 M / 6 s segment = 5 M RPS.
  • Telemetry / heartbeats: 30 M × 1 event/15 s = 2 M events/s.
  • Chat/reactions: 1% of viewers reacting → 300 K msgs/s in spikes.
  • Storage (DVR): 2 h × ~1 GB/h × 8 ABR rungs × 5 audio tracks = ~80 GB rolling per event.

6. High-level architecture

[Stadium camera] │ SDI ▼ [Encoder/Packager (low-latency HLS, 2s seg)] │ push ▼ [Origin (S3 / object store)] ─► [Multi-CDN: Akamai + Cloudfront + Jio CDN] │ ▼ India ISPs [Viewers] Control plane: [Viewer] → [Edge GW] → [Playback Svc] → [Manifest Svc] ├─► [Auth/Geo Svc] (rights enforcement) ├─► [Heartbeat Svc] → [Kafka] → [Flink] → concurrency dashboard └─► [Reactions Svc] → [Pulsar] → fanout to chat clients

The single most important decision: multi-CDN with active steering. No single CDN in India has the headroom for 75 Tbps; manifest URLs are signed by a CDN-selector that watches per-CDN error rate and rebalances every 30 s.

7. Data model & storage

7.1 Live session state (Redis Cluster)

HSET sess:{userId} eventId={..} cdn={..} abr={..} startedAt={..}
EXPIRE sess:{userId} 7200

Redis is in-memory, sharded by userId hash, used for the rate-limit counters and CDN-affinity decisions. Hot keys (event-level counters) use a 256-shard split and a periodic flusher.

7.2 VOD metadata (Cassandra)

CREATE TABLE titles (
  title_id uuid PRIMARY KEY,
  language text, genre text,
  drm_keys map<text,text>,
  manifest_path text
);

7.3 Telemetry warehouse

Heartbeats land in Kafka → Flink rolls up to per-second concurrency, per-CDN error rate, per-ISP throughput. This drives the CDN steering decisions in real time.

8. Detailed component design

8.1 Low-latency live pipeline

LL-HLS with 2-second segments and partial segments (CMAF chunks) brings glass-to-glass under 10 s when conditions allow; we accept up to 30 s for stability. The packager produces a single mezzanine and per-CDN-prefixed manifests so we can flip CDN by rewriting one URL.

8.2 Concurrency surge handling

At "toss" the system pre-warms by pushing the first 30 segments to all CDNs and pre-resolving DNS for the predicted user pool. Clients use a "cold-start" endpoint that returns a manifest already shifted to the least-loaded CDN.

8.3 Reactions / chat

Apache Pulsar with geo-partitioned topics; each region has its own writers and a global aggregator emits the trending counter back to all regions every 2 s (eventual consistency is fine, the user doesn't notice ±2 s on the meter).

9. Scaling & bottlenecks

  • Last-mile collapse: Indian ISPs saturate at certain peering points; we negotiate direct on-net caches with Jio, Airtel, and large MSOs.
  • CDN failover: client SDK has a circuit breaker — if 3 segments in 10 s fail, it requests a new manifest from the alternate CDN.
  • Heartbeat storm: 2 M RPS into a single Kafka topic — partition by user_id hash across 200 partitions, batch 500 ms on client side.
  • Chat fanout: capped at "top 20 reactions" + numeric counter rather than full message broadcast to avoid O(N²) fanout.

12. Talking points for the interviewer

  • "For an event-shaped load curve, the system has to be designed for the peak hour, not the average — autoscale doesn't save you when ramp is 30 M in 30 minutes."
  • "The hardest part is last-mile in India, not encoding; that's why on-net ISP caches and aggressive ABR floors matter more than fancy codecs."
  • "I'd run a chaos drill the week before the final, deliberately failing one CDN to validate the steering."
