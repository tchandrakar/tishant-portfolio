Design UPI (unified payments interface, NPCI-style)

Very Hard payments

1. Problem statement

Build the switch behind India's UPI: a real-time interbank rail where any payer at Bank A can push money to any payee at Bank B using a virtual address (vyom@okhdfcbank) in under 3 seconds, 24×7, with bank-grade settlement. NPCI sits in the middle as a switch + directory + dispute resolver. UPI does ~10 B transactions/month (2025) — roughly 4 K TPS average, 12–15 K TPS peak.

2. Functional requirements

  • VPA registration & resolution: map handle@bank → account number + IFSC.
  • Pay (push): payer-initiated transfer with PIN auth on payer's device.
  • Collect (pull): payee requests money; payer approves on their app.
  • Auto-pay mandates (recurring); QR static + dynamic; UPI Lite (offline small-value).
  • Dispute & reversal flow within T+1.
  • End-to-end E2EE PIN block; H2H links to every member bank.

3. Non-functional requirements

  • Latency: p99 < 3 s end-to-end; switch internal < 200 ms.
  • Scale: 10 B txns/month; peak 15 K TPS; 350 M+ users; 600+ member banks.
  • Availability: 99.99%; member banks may flap, switch must not.
  • Correctness: debit & credit are atomic across two independent banks; no money in flight after reconciliation.
  • Settlement: deferred net settlement (DNS) at RBI 4× daily.

4. Capacity estimation

  • Peak 15 K TPS × 5 messages (req, debit, credit, ack, settle) ≈ 75 K msgs/s through the switch.
  • Storage: 10 B txns/month × 12 × 1 KB × 7 yr retention ≈ 850 TB raw, 2.5 PB replicated.
  • VPA directory: 350 M VPAs × 256 B = 90 GB; lookups ~30 K QPS, < 20 ms p99 (Redis + Postgres).
  • Net settlement file: 600 banks × pairwise nets = ~360 K position rows × 4 cycles/day.

5. API design (ISO 20022-ish)

POST /upi/v1/pay             {payerVPA, payeeVPA, amount, currency=INR,
                              orderRef, deviceFP, pinBlock, idempotencyKey}
                             → {rrn, status: SUCCESS|PENDING|FAILED, errCode}
POST /upi/v1/collect         {payerVPA, payeeVPA, amount, expiresIn}
GET  /upi/v1/resolve/{vpa}   → {accountHash, ifsc, name, active}
POST /upi/v1/reversal        {originalRRN, reason}

6. High-level architecture

[Payer App] [Payee App] │ ▲ ▼ (TLS, signed PIN block) │ [Payer PSP] ──► [NPCI Switch] ◄────── [Payee PSP] │ │ │ │ │ │ ┌──────────┼───────────┐ │ │ │ ▼ ▼ ▼ │ │ │ [VPA Dir] [Risk] [Idempotency Store] │ │ │ │ │ │ ▼ ▼ ▼ │ [Payer Bank] ─── debit ───►[Switch]─── credit ──►[Payee Bank]┘ (Core CBS) (Core CBS) │ ▼ [Settlement Engine] (4× daily DNS at RBI)

Each leg (debit, credit) is a synchronous H2H call with strict 30-second timeout. The switch is the orchestrator + system of record for the txn lifecycle; banks are participants.

7. Data model & storage

7.1 Transaction journal (Postgres, sharded by RRN prefix)

CREATE TABLE upi_txn (
  rrn          CHAR(12) PRIMARY KEY,         -- retrieval ref number
  payer_vpa    TEXT, payee_vpa TEXT,
  amount       NUMERIC(15,2),
  state        SMALLINT,  -- INIT, DEBITED, CREDITED, CLOSED, REVERSED
  payer_bank   CHAR(11), payee_bank CHAR(11),
  created_at   TIMESTAMPTZ,
  closed_at    TIMESTAMPTZ,
  idem_key     TEXT UNIQUE
);
CREATE INDEX ix_state_time ON upi_txn(state, created_at) WHERE state < 3;

7.2 Why Postgres for the journal?

The state machine needs serializable updates: a debit ack and a timeout reversal must not both run. SSI (DDIA Ch.7) makes the predicate "no other writer for this rrn" cheap. CockroachDB / Spanner-style if multi-region active-active is needed.

8. Detailed component design

8.1 The two-leg saga

UPI is fundamentally a saga because the payer bank and payee bank are independent systems we can't 2PC-lock. Sequence:

1. NPCI receives PAY → write INIT row (idem-keyed)
2. Call payer bank: DEBIT (synchronous, 7 s budget)
   - on SUCCESS: state=DEBITED
   - on TIMEOUT/UNKNOWN: state=DEBITED_PENDING, reconcile via batch
3. Call payee bank: CREDIT (synchronous, 7 s budget)
   - on SUCCESS: state=CREDITED → emit ack to payer
   - on FAILURE: enqueue COMPENSATING_REVERSAL → re-credit payer bank
4. Final state CLOSED only after credit ack (or reversal ack)

8.2 Idempotency & replay safety

Every request carries (deviceFP, txnId). The switch enforces a unique constraint on (payer_psp, txnId). Bank-side, the debit message carries the RRN; banks must dedupe by RRN. This is the ONLY way to get exactly-once-ish semantics on a non-2PC rail (DDIA Ch.7 idempotent operations).

8.3 Settlement

The switch journal is the source of truth. Every CLOSED txn flows to the settlement engine, which nets per-bank-pair amounts. At T+0 4× a day, NPCI posts net positions to RBI's RTGS for inter-bank settlement. Reconciliation files (raw tape) are exchanged with each bank within 30 minutes of cycle close.

9. Scaling & bottlenecks

  • Slow bank drags the switch: per-bank circuit breaker + bulkhead thread pools so HDFC's flap doesn't starve SBI traffic.
  • Hot VPA directory: Redis cluster with read-replicas; LRU + 60s TTL; cold-path Postgres.
  • Festival peaks (Diwali, IPL): autoscale stateless app tier; pre-shard journal partitions; throttle non-essential collects to protect pay traffic.
  • Reversal storm: if a bank is down post-debit, queued reversals can stampede on recovery — token-bucket per bank.

11. DDIA references

  • Ch. 7 Transactions — UPI is the textbook case for "sagas instead of distributed transactions"; idempotency keys, single-object atomic state transitions, and SSI on the txn journal are all directly applicable.
  • Ch. 7 Transactions — the "what does it mean to fail?" discussion: UNKNOWN state after a network timeout is a first-class concept here, not an edge case.
  • Ch. 9 Consensus — total order broadcast for settlement file generation; Raft for switch-leader election per shard so reversals aren't double-fired.
  • Ch. 9 Consensus — linearizable VPA-resolve reads (a stale VPA → wrong account = financial loss).
  • Ch. 11 Stream Processing — Kafka journal for downstream analytics, fraud, and dispute systems.

12. Talking points for the interviewer

  • "UPI's brilliance is the indirection: VPA decouples identity from account, so the switch can be agnostic of CBS schemas."
  • "The hardest engineering problem is not throughput; it's the UNKNOWN state — a debited-but-not-credited txn — and the reconciliation discipline that closes it."
  • "I'd never pretend this is exactly-once; it's at-least-once with bank-side dedup by RRN, and we sleep at night because of the daily recon file."

13. Tech stack & rationale

Every technology choice in this design exists for a concrete reason.

TechnologyWhy we chose it (for THIS problem)Alternatives considered
Postgres (SSI) — txn journal 15 K TPS peak × 5 state messages each = 75 K msg/s on the journal. SSI (DDIA Ch.7) prevents the race where a debit-ack and a timeout-reversal both fire on the same RRN — write skew is not theoretical here, it would cause double reversals on flaky bank links. MySQL (no proper SSI); CockroachDB (kept on the table for active-active multi-region v2).
Spanner / CockroachDB — multi-region option NPCI's switch is single-region today, but if we ever go active-active across Mumbai & Hyderabad, we need TrueTime/HLC-style external consistency for RRN ordering (DDIA Ch.9). 600+ banks pinging both DCs would race without it. Postgres logical replication (asymmetric writes hard); MySQL Group Replication (eventual consistency on conflicts).
Cassandra — explicitly rejected for journal The journal's state machine (INIT → DEBITED → CREDITED → CLOSED) needs CAS on the rrn row that is guaranteed not to lose a write. Cassandra's LWW would silently overwrite a CREDITED back to DEBITED on a clock skew — unacceptable. Used only for downstream analytics dump. n/a — this is the rejection.
Kafka — downstream events & recon Every CLOSED txn fans out to fraud, dispute, settlement, analytics. 75 K msg/s peak × 7-day retention = ~45 TB; Kafka's per-partition ordering preserves RRN sequence so settlement nets correctly (DDIA Ch.11). RabbitMQ (no replay window, weak ordering at scale); Pulsar (less ops familiarity in NPCI's stack).
Flink — fraud / velocity features Velocity rules (">5 transfers from same VPA in 60s") need windowed stateful aggregations at 15 K TPS. Flink's checkpointed RocksDB state (DDIA Ch.11) survives switch failover without losing in-flight windows; latency budget for fraud is < 200 ms. Kafka Streams (state limits); Spark Streaming (micro-batch latency too high for inline).
Redis — VPA directory cache 30 K QPS resolves with < 20 ms p99 budget; 350 M VPAs × 256 B = 90 GB easily fits a Redis cluster. LRU + 60 s TTL absorbs the festival-peak burst when everyone is collecting payments simultaneously. Memcached (no replication, lossy on failover); local cache (no consistency across switch nodes).
Saga (two-leg debit/credit) Payer Bank A and Payee Bank B are independently regulated CBS systems — we cannot 2PC-lock across them. Saga with explicit DEBITED_PENDING / COMPENSATING_REVERSAL states is the only correct shape (DDIA Ch.9: 2PC fails when participants are autonomous orgs). Distributed 2PC (XA) — politically and technically impossible across banks; Synchronous mirroring (banks would never sign off).
2PC — explicitly rejected 2PC across 600+ banks would mean every bank's CBS holds locks waiting on every other; one bank's GC pause stalls the rail. Saga + bank-side dedup by RRN is the practical at-least-once contract NPCI actually runs on. Saga (chosen); per-pair clearing house (operational nightmare).
Idempotency keys — (payer_psp, txnId) The switch enforces a UNIQUE constraint on (payer_psp, txnId); banks dedupe by RRN. This is the ONLY mechanism that prevents double-debit when a payer app retries after a timeout (DDIA Ch.7: "exactly-once = at-least-once + idempotent"). Switch-only dedup (banks would still double-debit); Bank-only dedup (switch would re-issue conflicting RRNs).
Double-entry settlement journal Net settlement at RBI requires bank-pair positions that always sum to zero across the system; double-entry on the switch journal makes net = sum(credits) − sum(debits) per bank pair, mathematically reconcilable against bank tape files. Single-sided ledger (cannot prove conservation across bank boundaries during recon).
Raft (etcd) — switch shard leader Each journal shard has a Raft-elected leader so reversals aren't double-fired during failover (DDIA Ch.9). 5 s lease, 200 ms internal SLA easily covered. ZooKeeper (older, heavier); database-led election (slower failover).
Per-bank circuit breaker / bulkhead 15 K TPS comes from 600 banks unevenly; if HDFC's link goes flap, its requests must not consume thread-pools meant for SBI. Bulkhead pattern + token-bucket per bank stops the failure from cascading switch-wide. Global queue (one bad bank starves all); naive timeout (head-of-line blocking).
Reconciliation files (T+0 4× daily) UNKNOWN states (debited but no credit-ack) are the dominant correctness risk on UPI. The recon file (raw tape from each bank) is compared against the journal within 30 min of cycle close — closes the gap that no inline mechanism can. Pure inline reconciliation (impossible — bank CBS state isn't fully visible in real-time).
